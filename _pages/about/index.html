Since August 2021, I am a graduate student in Economics at the University of Oklahoma, where I am currently a Ph.D. candidate and graduate assistant under the supervision of Prof. Firat Demir.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a National Consultant at the International Trade Center (UN/WTO) (March 2018 – July 2021). I completed my Masters in Economics at University of Neuchâtel in 2018 and Bachelors at Christ University in 2014.

My areas of research are International Economics, Environmental Economics and Development Economics. I am particularly interested in studying how economic policy spillovers transcend national boundaries, with a focus on the intersection of trade and environment.

My research has been recognized with Alexander J. Kondonassis Award for research in Development Economics. I have taught undergraduate Microeconomics and Macroeconomics at the University of Oklahoma, Islington College (London Metropolitan University), and Apex College (Pokhara University).
